# Product Scope

## Product Definition

SB Page helps people who do not know how websites work create the simplest credible page for their business.

The launch promise is a live business page in under 10 minutes. The page should answer four customer questions:

1. Who is this business?
2. What does it offer?
3. How can I contact or visit it?
4. What should I do next?

The primary customer is a solo owner or very small team, including service businesses, food and drink businesses, makers, retailers, professionals, and appointment-based local businesses.

## Core Product Rules

- Guide the owner instead of presenting a blank canvas.
- Use business language instead of web-design language.
- Keep one primary decision on each screen.
- Mark optional information clearly and allow it to be skipped.
- Never silently lose work.
- Keep saved work separate from the live page.
- Show the result before asking the owner to publish or pay.
- Make meaningful actions reversible.
- Hide advanced choices until they are needed.
- Treat mobile as the primary experience.

## Current Repository Milestone

This initial milestone includes only:

- Next.js App Router setup
- TypeScript, Tailwind CSS, ESLint, Prettier, and npm scripts
- A minimal mobile-first page that proves the application runs
- Foundation tests
- Product and engineering documentation

No product feature is considered implemented by this milestone.

## Approved Launch Product Scope

The Figma strategy defines the intended launch product. Future tasks may implement these items in the documented sequence:

- Account access and recovery
- Guided onboarding and page-name selection
- A page-first owner workspace
- Top section, offers, About, photos, details, and external links
- Header and footer controls
- Mobile and desktop preview
- Saved and live versions with explicit publishing and rollback
- One paid subscription and a trial
- A small permissioned operator console
- Product analytics, monitoring, and support tooling

The presence of an item in this list does not authorize building it during the foundation milestone.

## Later Only If Proven

- AI writing suggestions
- More visual templates
- Customer traffic reporting
- Custom domains
- Additional staff accounts
- More media and themes
- Agency or multi-business accounts

These require evidence from real customers and a separate approval.

## Explicitly Not V1

- Native scheduling or calendar software
- SMS inboxes or phone service
- Email hosting or business email
- Blogging or long-form publishing
- Full ecommerce or checkout
- CRM, marketing automation, or loyalty programs
- Multiple locations
- Custom CSS or advanced freeform layout
- An open-ended drag-and-drop builder

## External Customer Actions

Booking, ordering, shopping, phone, text, email, maps, social profiles, and review destinations remain external in V1. SB Page helps the owner present these destinations clearly; it does not replace them.

## Success Measures

The product strategy proposes these targets for later validation:

- 80 percent of new owners complete onboarding.
- Median time from account creation to first live page is under 10 minutes.
- 75 percent publish without contacting support.
- Fewer than 1 support contact occurs per 10 activations.
- 70 percent of paid pages remain live after 90 days.

No analytics system is connected yet, so these are product targets rather than current measurements.
